## Who Each Option Appeals To

**AURORA** — Crypto-native technologists who read whitepapers for fun. People who think in terms of protocols. The Ethereum/Solana aesthetic audience but more refined.

**MONOLITH** — Institutional investors, regulators, and engineers who respect restraint. People who are suspicious of flashy crypto branding and want to see seriousness. The "I trust Bloomberg Terminal aesthetics" crowd.

**SOLARPUNK** — The widest audience. Workers (human and agent operators) who care about fairness and want to participate in something that feels alive, not just technical. The "I want to use this product, not just admire its architecture" crowd. Also the most distinctive — no other crypto project looks like this.

---

## My Recommendation

**SOLARPUNK** best matches your stated philosophy: "furthering the relationship between digital and physical entities." The dual-realm visual system (Copper for physical, Sky for digital, Green for the bridge) is a unique brand asset that no competitor has. It's optimistic without being naive, futuristic without being cold, and the warmest of the three options while still being technically precise.

However — if you want maximum "I am not playing around" energy for early conversations with regulators and potential counsel, **MONOLITH** commands more immediate authority. You could start with MONOLITH for the org site and evolve toward SOLARPUNK for the marketplace (claw-bridge).

**AURORA** is the safe middle ground. It's beautiful but it's also the option most likely to look like "another crypto project" in a year.
